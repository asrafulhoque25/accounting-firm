Most accounting firm websites quietly lose qualified prospects

No service tier transparency

Prospects can't tell if your firm is built for their size. A $400K-revenue dental practice and a $50M manufacturing company need different services, but most accounting sites describe everything as 'comprehensive solutions.' Tiered pricing pages (or at minimum 'starting at' ranges by client size) cut tire-kicker inquiries by half.

Credentials hidden or missing entirely

CPA license number, EA designation, state of admission, MAcc or specialty certifications — these are the single most important trust signals for an accounting prospect. We see them buried in a partner bio paragraph or missing entirely on roughly 60% of the firm sites we audit.

Generic Services page where industries live

A CPA who works with restaurants, dental practices, and SaaS companies needs three separate landing pages — each targeting that industry's specific search behavior. One 'Services' page collapses all of that intent and ranks for none of it.

No secure document portal visibility

Prospects who've been burned by an email-attachment-based firm look for ShareFile, SmartVault, or TaxDome integration before they reach out. If your site doesn't mention the secure portal, prospects assume you don't have one and call the firm that does.

Tax deadline panic without a countdown

January through April is 60–70% of annual lead volume for tax-heavy firms. A visible deadline countdown widget (January 31 W-2/1099 deadline, March 15 S-corp deadline, April 15 individual deadline) drives seasonal urgency that most firm sites leave on the table.

No client onboarding pathway visible

First-time prospects don't know what to expect. 'What happens after I fill out the consultation form?' is the unspoken question that kills most form submissions. A visible 4-step onboarding ('Discovery call → Engagement letter → Document collection → First deliverable') answers it.

Slow consultation response time

Internal data from 12 firms we've worked with: prospects who don't hear back within 4 hours of submitting a contact form convert at 1/3 the rate. Most accounting sites send a generic auto-response and the partner doesn't see the email until Tuesday morning. CallRail with text-back integration fixes this.

Generic blog full of 'Tax Tip Tuesday' content

Every accounting blog has the same '5 tax tips for small business owners' post. None of them rank. SEO content for accountants has to target real questions ('How does an S-corp pay an owner-employee?' 'What expenses can I deduct as a 1099 contractor in California?') with depth no generalist blog has.

Nine essential features CPAs & bookkeepers actually use

Service tier comparison built into the homepage

Bronze, Silver, Gold (or starter/growth/enterprise) tier pricing with what's included at each level. Filters out tire-kickers, qualifies inquiries, and shortcuts the 'are you the right size for us?' question that kills most discovery calls.

Credentials display in the header

CPA license number, state of admission, EA designation, AICPA membership, specialty certifications (CFE, CMA, MAcc). Visible in the header or hero, not buried three pages deep where prospects never look.

Secure client portal link prominently displayed

ShareFile, SmartVault, TaxDome, Canopy — whatever portal you use, the link lives in the header. Signals security to new prospects and shortcuts the login flow for existing clients.

Industry-specific landing pages

Restaurants, dental practices, medical, real estate investors, nonprofits, SaaS — each industry gets its own page targeting that industry's search behavior, pain points, and regulatory context (R&D credits, 1099 issues, multi-state nexus, 990 compliance).

Tax deadline countdown widget

Visible countdown to next major deadline (January 31, March 15, April 15, June 15 estimated, September 15 extended S-corp/partnership, October 15 extended individual). Drives the 'I should book this consultation now' urgency.

Free calculator or assessment lead magnet

Withholding calculator, estimated tax calculator, entity structure comparison, or QuickBooks health check — a free tool that captures the email of a prospect who's researching the problem before they look for a firm.

Consultation intake questionnaire on the form

Revenue range, entity type, current accountant status, biggest current pain — captured at the form stage so the first call is real work, not 'tell me about your business' over again. Higher friction, better leads.

Onboarding pathway visualization

A 4-step graphic showing what happens after the consultation request — engagement letter, document collection, kickoff meeting, first deliverable. Answers the silent objection that kills form submissions.

What we won't promise

Three honest paths. Each fits a different firm. Here's the math, no spin.

Realistic local SEO ranking expectations

Page-1 rankings on day one for 'CPA [major metro].' 'CPA NYC' has been fought over for 20 years. We can reliably rank you for niche-plus-neighborhood combinations ('SaaS CPA Brooklyn,' 'construction accountant Queens') 60–120 days out.

Winning against big accounting competitors

Outranking Intuit, H&R Block, or the directory aggregators. They've spent millions on SEO. We can win the local 3-pack and industry-specific niches they don't cover well.

Compliant accounting marketing approach

Results without bar and AICPA-compliant marketing language. AICPA Code of Professional Conduct restricts certain comparative claims. State boards add their own rules. We follow them. No 'best in [state]' or 'guaranteed lower taxes' anywhere on the site.

Understanding seasonal accounting demand

Outcomes during the off-season at peak-season volume. May–December consultation volume is structurally lower than January–April. The site does its job year-round, but tax-season is tax-season.

Frequently asked questions

Answers to the most common questions from accounting firm owners about pricing, process, and results

Q

Is the $999 price really all-in?

Yes. Logo, branding, accounting firm website, SEO content, on-page SEO setup, Google Business Profile optimization, and 30 days of post-launch support. No hidden fees. Practice management software (QuickBooks, Xero, TaxDome) and tax prep software (Drake, Lacerte, UltraTax) are your existing tools — not part of the package and not something we sell.

Q

Will my firm rank #1 for 'CPA [my city]'?

No one can honestly promise a #1 ranking. What we can do is rank you for realistic niche-plus-neighborhood combinations and move you into the local 3-pack over 60–120 days, instead of fighting 20-year-old competition for the single most contested term.

Q

How do you handle AICPA and state board compliance?

Every page follows the AICPA Code of Professional Conduct and state board advertising rules. No 'best in [state]' or 'guaranteed lower taxes' language. Comparative claims stay factual and defensible.

Q

Do you integrate with TaxDome, Canopy, Karbon, or ShareFile?

Yes. We add a prominent portal link in the header so new prospects see the security signal and existing clients get a fast login. Whatever portal you use works.

Q

What if I already have a site on Squarespace, Wix, or an old WordPress build?

We rebuild from scratch on WordPress (or Webflow), migrate the content worth keeping, and set up redirects so you don't lose the rankings you already have.

Q

Do you do ongoing SEO or content writing after launch?

The package includes launch content and 30 days of support. Ongoing content is an optional add-on at $150–300 per post — no forced retainer.

Q

Can you build separate landing pages for each industry I serve?

Yes. Each industry gets its own page targeting that industry's search behavior and pain points, instead of one generic 'Services' page that ranks for nothing.

Q

What happens after the 30-day support window?

You own the site, content, and domain outright. Maintain it yourself, hand it to any developer, or add our optional content plan. No lock-in.

Q

How does this compare to FindLaw or accounting-specific SaaS marketing platforms?

Those are templated, rented platforms — you never own the site and you pay monthly forever. We build a custom site you own outright for a one-time fee.

Q

Can the site handle tax season urgency messaging?

Yes. We build in deadline countdowns and seasonal CTAs so the site drives urgency January–April, then shifts to advisory and planning content the rest of the year.
